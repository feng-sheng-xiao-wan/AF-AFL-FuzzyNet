To preliminarily assess whether the proposed framework can operate under reduced-lead ECG input, we conducted a lead-configuration comparison on the same PTB-XL AF/AFL subset. The full 12-lead setting used all standard ECG leads, whereas the reduced two-lead setting used only leads II and V1. Both settings were evaluated on the same ECG records using the same preprocessing and evaluation protocol. The II+V1 setting achieved an AFL-F1 of 0.902, compared with 0.892 for the 12-lead setting. A paired bootstrap analysis yielded a mean AFL-F1 difference of 0.010 with a 95% CI of [-0.017, 0.035]. These results provide preliminary evidence that the proposed AF/AFL classification framework can operate with reduced-lead ECG input, although larger-scale multi-lead validation is needed before claiming formal equivalence to 12-lead ECG.
